Performing Arts at Washington High School

Orchestra

String Orchestra

Director, Natalie Brown String Orchestra meetings daily during 2nd hour. This ensemble is composed of primarily 9th grade students.

Alternative Strings

Instructor, Natalie Brown Alternative Strings meets after school once a week. The group provides opportunities for young string players to focus on technique and repertoire from American and world traditions, creative musicianship in all styles, and technology-enhanced approaches. Alternative styles such as jazz, folk and popular music offer students exposure to a variety of rhythm structures, techniques and expressive elements of music. Opportunities for performance in the community will be available.

Symphony Orchestra

Director, Natalie Brown Symphony Orchestra meets daily during 1st hour. Symphony includes primarily 10th-12th grade string students and occasionally select wind, brass and percussion students.

Band

Marching Band

The Marching Band is composed of student in grades 9-12. The season begins with a focused multi-day band camp during mid-August at IO-DIS-E-CA, south of Cedar Rapids, followed a succession of day-long stadium camps and stadium rehearsals (all at Kingston Stadium). Performances include football games, parades, and contests. The Marching Band enjoys a decades-long history of success and regularly competes against the best bands from all over the region at various marching band contests.

Concert Band

The Concert Band is composed of beginning to intermediate band students as determined by auditions conducted after the conclusion of the Marching Band season.

Wind Symphony

The Wind Symphony is composed of advanced band students as determined by auditions conducted after the conclusion of the Marching Band season.

Revolutionists

The Revolutionists are composed of advanced jazz band students as determined by auditions normally conducted during early September. The Revolutionists meet for practice outside of the normal hours of the school day. The ensemble routinely learns over 20 pieces of jazz band music each year, drawing from traditional standard repertoire and new compositions. The Revolutionists have qualified for ten State Jazz Band Championships in a row, where they have placed at the top each time. In addition they are a frequent winners at other competitions around the state and were the featured jazz band at the 2006 and 2009 Iowa Bandmasters Association Conventions.

Jazz Two

Jazz Two is composed of intermediate jazz band students. Jazz Two meets for practice outside of the normal hours of the school day and performs and competes at numerous venues in Cedar Rapids and eastern Iowa. In a chamber group (one person per part) such as a jazz band, perfect attendance from every member is crucial to the success of the group.
